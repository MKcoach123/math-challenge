Week 7 — Answers & Solutions

Grade 4 · Math Challenge
Problem 1
Correct time on the mirrored clock?
Answer: 8:15
Mark the numbers on the mirror image, then flip it back so it reads normally. Now you can see the time is 8:15.
Problem 2
How many cats?
Answer: 5
There are 12 pets altogether (12 tails). Pretend they are all cats: that would be 12 × 4 = 48 legs. But there are only 34, so we have 48 − 34 = 14 legs too many. A cat has 4 − 2 = 2 more legs than a parrot, so we turn 14 ÷ 2 = 7 cats into parrots. That leaves 12 − 7 = 5 cats and 7 parrots.
Check: 5 × 4 + 7 × 2 = 20 + 14 = 34 legs. ✓
Problem 3
What number replaces the question mark?
Answer: 10
From the first balance, one full circle = two stars. Put that into the second balance: 3 stars = 30, so 1 star = 10. The third balance has half a circle = one star = 10.
Problem 4
Which construction was it before?
Answer: E
Add the knocked-off block back and compare with each option — only E matches.
Problem 5
How many dots from the 1st to the 6th pattern?
Answer: 56
The patterns hold 1, 3, 6, 10, 15, 21 dots. Add them up: 1 + 3 + 6 + 10 + 15 + 21 = 56.
Problem 6
How many meters does the cat walk?
Answer: 83
The cat walks along the centre line, so for each turn we use half the corridor width: 8 ÷ 2 = 4, (40 − 36) ÷ 2 = 2, 6 ÷ 2 = 3.• Bottom corridor: 40 − 2 = 38 m
• Up the middle: 20 − 4 + 3 = 19 m
• Top corridor: 28 − 2 = 26 m
Total: 38 + 19 + 26 = 83 m.
