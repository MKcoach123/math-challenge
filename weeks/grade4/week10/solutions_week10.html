Week 10 — Answers & Solutions

Grade 4 · Math Challenge
Problem 1
Smallest number of jars to be sure of one honey jar?
Answer: 19
Honey is the rarest — only 5 jars. Imagine Pooh is very unlucky and pulls out every other jar first: all 12 jam + 6 peanut butter = 18 jars, and still no honey. The 19th jar must then be honey. So 18 tries isn't enough, but 19 guarantees it.
Problem 2
Will the gears turn, or are they jammed?
Answer: Jammed
Say the biggest gear turns clockwise, then follow the loop marking each gear. Two gears that touch must spin in opposite directions — but the gears form a closed ring of 9 (an odd number), so going all the way around you reach a gear that would have to spin both ways at once. That's impossible, so the system is jammed.
Problem 3
How many of the numbers are even?
Answer: 5
Even numbers end in 0, 2, 4, 6, or 8. Checking the list, the even ones are 234, 1112, 1236, 3118, and 100000000002 — that's 5 numbers.
Problem 4
What page does Ellie stop on?
Answer: 111
Watch the small cases first. If Ellie read only 3 pages she'd read pages 62, 63, 64 — that's 62 + 3 − 1 = 64. If she read 10 pages she'd stop on 62 + 10 − 1 = 71. We subtract 1 because page 62 itself is the first page she reads. So for 50 pages: 62 + 50 − 1 = 111.
Problem 5
How many cubes are in the structure?
Answer: 19
Count it layer by layer from the top down: 1 + 3 + 6 + 9 = 19 cubes.
Problem 6
How many more 3-gram weights for the car than the ball?
Answer: 7
The car is heavier than the ball by 66 − 45 = 21 grams. To make up 21 grams using 3-gram weights, Juliana needs 21 ÷ 3 = 7 more weights.
