Week 3 — Answers & Solutions

Grade 4 · Math Challenge
Problem 1
How many squares are in the picture?
Answer: 10
Count by size:
• squares made of 1 cell: 8
• squares made of 4 cells (2×2): 2
8 + 2 = 10.
Problem 2
How many triangles are in the picture?
Answer: 8
Count by size:
• triangles made of 1 small triangle: 3
• triangles made of 2 small triangles: 4
• the big triangle (all of them): 1
3 + 4 + 1 = 8.
Problem 3
How many rectangles are in the picture?
Answer: 16
Count by width:
• rectangles 1 cell wide: 7
• rectangles 2 cells wide: 6
• rectangles 3 cells wide: 3
7 + 6 + 3 = 16.
Problem 4
How many cubes are there in the object?
Answer: 9
Count the cubes layer by layer (top → bottom):
1 + 3 + 5 = 9.
Problem 5
John placed pebbles on a table 2 cm apart. How many pebbles did he place over a distance of 20 cm?
Answer: 11
Start small: 2 pebbles span 2 cm, 3 pebbles span 4 cm — each extra 2 cm adds one more pebble.
For 20 cm: after the first 2 cm there are 20 − 2 = 18 cm left → 18 ÷ 2 = 9 more pebbles.
2 + 9 = 11.
