Week 6 — Answers & Solutions

Grade 4 · Math Challenge
Problem 1
Which gears turn clockwise with gear 2?
Answer: Gear 4
Gear 2 turns clockwise, so every gear touching it turns the opposite way (counter-clockwise), and the next gear turns clockwise again. Following the chain, the only other gear turning clockwise is gear 4.
Problem 2
How much does the headband cost?
Answer: $6
The 2 scrunchies cost 2 × $2 = $4. Charlotte spent $10 in total, so the headband costs 10 − 4 = $6.
Problem 3
Sum of squares A and B?
Answer: 4
Fill the grid so each row and column has 1, 2 and 3 once. In row 1 the last square must be 2, so the middle is 3. Working through, A = 1 and B = 3, so A + B = 4.
Problem 4
Points on the third turn?
Answer: 21
First turn: all three arrows hit the same outer ring for 12, so each outer hit = 12 ÷ 3 = 4. Second turn: two outer hits + one centre = 15, so the centre = 15 − 2×4 = 7. Third turn: all three in the centre = 3 × 7 = 21.
Problem 5
How many space chickens?
Answer: 10
A good way to start is to guess that half the animals are chickens and half are rabbits — so 15 of each. Then count the legs (a chicken has 2 legs, a rabbit has 4):
No of chickensNo of ch legsNo of rabbitsNo of rabbit legsTotal
1530156090
That makes only 90 legs, but the farmer has 100. We are 100 − 90 = 10 legs short.

A rabbit has 4 − 2 = 2 more legs than a chicken. So every time we change one chicken into one rabbit, the total number of legs goes up by 2.
To make up the 10 missing legs we change 10 ÷ 2 = 5 animals — take away 5 chickens and add 5 rabbits:
No of chickensNo of ch legsNo of rabbitsNo of rabbit legsTotal
10202080100
Now the legs add up to 100, so the farmer has 10 space chickens.
Problem 6
Who is the youngest?
Answer: Mike
Put everyone on an age line from the clues: Audrey is older than Athena; Athena is older than Esper, who is older than Rayan; and Rayan is older than Mike. So the order is Audrey > Athena > Esper > Rayan > Mike — the youngest is Mike.
