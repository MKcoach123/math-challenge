Problem 1
How many squares are in the 5th pattern?
Answer: 9
Each shape has 2 more squares than the one before it:
Shape 1: 1
Shape 2: 1 + 2 = 3
Shape 3: 3 + 2 = 5
Shape 4: 5 + 2 = 7
Shape 5: 7 + 2 = 9.
Problem 2
Fewest L-tiles to cover the 6×6 floor?
Answer: 12
The tile is made of 3 squares. The floor is 6 × 6 = 36 squares.
To cover it: 36 ÷ 3 = 12 tiles.

Problem 4
Missing number: 24, 3, 21, 6, 18, ?
Answer: 9
Two patterns take turns. The numbers in the odd spots go 24, 21, 18 (−3 each), and the numbers in the even spots go 3, 6, ? (+3 each).
The missing number is the next even-spot number: 6 + 3 = 9.
(so the full row is 24, 3, 21, 6, 18, 9, 15)
Problem 5
Which digit does the triangle cover?
Answer: 9
The result is 4□ and △5 − 48 = 4□. The only digit that works is △ = 9, since 95 − 48 = 47 (so □ = 7).
