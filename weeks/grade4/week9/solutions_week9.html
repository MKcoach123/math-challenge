Problem 3
In how many ways can you form a 3-digit number using 1, 2 and 3 once each?
Answer: 6
Just list them all: 123, 132, 213, 231, 312, 321 — that's 6 numbers (3 × 2 × 1 = 6).
Problem 4
Is the sum of the first 5 even numbers odd or even?
Answer: Even
The sum of two even numbers is even, so the sum of any number of even numbers stays even. Indeed 2 + 4 + 6 + 8 + 10 = 30, which is even.
Problem 5
How many of the designs can Torey make?
Answer: 4
Each tile covers one green and one pink square, so a design can be made only if it splits into such tiles. Picture number 2 cannot be made this way (it has more green squares than pink). The other four work, so Torey can make 4 of the designs.
Problem 6
Which element belongs in place of the question mark?
Answer: 4
Count the light (green) parts row by row: the first row has one, the second row has two, the third row has three — and the pattern also shifts by one part each step. The only option that continues the pattern is number 4.
