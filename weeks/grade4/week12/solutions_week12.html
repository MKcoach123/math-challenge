Week 12 — Answers & Solutions

Grade 4 · Math Challenge
Problem 1
Side of the square marked with the question mark?
Answer: 15
Start from the smallest square (side 3) and work outwards, finding each square's side in turn. The square marked “?” turns out to be 9 + 6 = 15.
Problem 2
What is Sarah's number?
Answer: 39
The digit pairs where one is 3 times the other are 1 & 3, 2 & 6, and 3 & 9, giving the two-digit numbers 13, 26 and 39. The only one greater than 30 is 39.
Problem 3
Which way does gear 6 turn?
Answer: Clockwise
Mark each gear's direction in turn starting from gear 1 (counter-clockwise), remembering that two meshed gears spin opposite ways while a belt makes the gears it links spin the same way. Following it all the way around, gear 6 turns clockwise.
Problem 4
Solve the cuneiform example.
Answer: 19
Reading the cuneiform: <<<V = 31 and <VV = 12, so 31 − 12 = 19.
Problem 5
Which picture does not belong?
Answer: 4
In every figure the number of stars matches the number of corners — except figure 4, where they don't match. So figure 4 is the one that doesn't belong.
Problem 6
How much does the heart weigh?
Answer: 3
The whole mobile weighs 72, so each side of the top bar is 72 ÷ 2 = 36. The left bar splits into 18 and 18; the three equal diamonds make 18 ÷ 3 = 6 each; the star is 36 ÷ 4 = 9. So the heart = 18 − 9 − 6 = 3.
